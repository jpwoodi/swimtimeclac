Building a Writing Habit as an Engineer

A small weekly workflow that helps me publish technical writing without turning it into a second full-time project.

I wanted to write more, but every article felt too big. I would start with a rough idea, open a blank page, and then lose momentum because I was trying to produce something perfect in one session.

The change that helped most was reducing scope. Instead of aiming for one long piece each month, I now publish one short article each week with one clear takeaway.

My Current System

On Monday, I capture three possible topics from work that week. On Wednesday, I choose one and sketch an outline with a problem, what I tried, and what I learned. On Friday, I publish the draft after one pass for clarity.

This structure keeps the barrier low. I can always improve older pieces later, but the habit only works if I keep shipping.

Why It Works

Short, frequent writing creates a feedback loop. Each article improves my thinking and gives me raw material for future, deeper posts. Over time, I am building a body of work rather than waiting for perfect conditions.
